Phase 1

What Type of Data Do You Have?

The first step in any system design interview or architecture meeting is understanding the nature of your data. Is it rigid like a bank ledger, flexible like a user profile, or massive like a video library?

üìä Structured

Highly organized data with a strict schema. Rows and columns. Think Excel sheets on steroids. Essential for financial records and inventory.

üìù Semistructured

Data that contains tags or markers to separate semantic elements but lacks a rigid structure. JSON, XML, emails. The playground of NoSQL.

üì¶ Unstructured

Information that either does not have a pre-defined data model or is not organized in a pre-defined manner. Videos, images, audio files.

Estimated distribution of data types in modern large-scale applications. Semistructured data dominates due to the flexibility of JSON APIs.

If your data is structured, the next critical question is the Use Case. Are you processing individual sales in real-time, or analyzing years of sales history?
